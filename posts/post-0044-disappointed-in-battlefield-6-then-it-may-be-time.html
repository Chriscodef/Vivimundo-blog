Disappointed in Battlefield 6? Then It May Be Time to Try This Free-to-Play FPS

Battlefield 6is only a few days away from dropping Season 2, and it hopefully proves exciting enough to bring forth some much-needed hype to the FPS. Last year,Battlefield 6was a record-breaking launch for the franchise, but it's also faced plenty of ups and downs since its strong opening weekend. Between hit detection issues, claims of not enough large-scale maps, controversial skins,RedSeccomplaints, and still no naval maps in sight,Battlefield 6has left a lot of players frustrated. Of course, there is still the chance forBattlefield 6to bounce back. The military shooter has only been active for a few months now, butBattlefield 6Season 2is going to have to bring some strong content if players are going to remain invested (or come back to the shooter). And whether a player is waiting for the game to improve or they're just ready to leaveBattlefield 6behind and invest in a different game, one free-to-play option could be the perfect placeholder.Delta Forcehas been out since 2024, and in that time it's proven to be an incredibly strong FPS that could fill the void someBattlefield 6players have been left with. These are the hallmark traits oftheBattlefieldfranchise, andDelta Forceboasts many of the same essential features while also being completely free. LikeBattlefield 6,Delta Forcehas a modern setting. So, for theBattlefieldfans who are still searching for an FPS set in WWI and WWII,Delta Forcemay not scratch the correct itch. However,Delta Forcecould be ideal for gamers wanting well-made large-scale battles that feature plenty of play style diversity.
