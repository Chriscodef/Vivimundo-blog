Steam Users Have Until February 8 to Claim 2025 Single-Player Survival Game Completely Free of Charge

Steamusers can currently claim the 2025 single-player survival gameArabian Gulfcompletely free of charge. Gamers looking to add this game to theirSteamlibraries must act fast, as this free promotion is only available until February 8. Steam has been offering plenty of free gamesof late, allowing players to bulk up their game libraries at no cost. These games don't have to be played immediately, so often gamers will grab them while they're free and add them to their growing backlogs. After all, it costs absolutely nothing. While the Epic Games Store is where PC gamers can often find recognizable titles for free, Steam's giveaways are often from indie developers looking to get eyes on their game. This is almost more exciting - indie games are often a little more experimental with their ideas, which can make playing them a far more interesting experience. If nothing else, grabbing these games while they're free is a consistent way to progress toward Steam's game collector badges, which can be displayed on public profiles. Valve reveals that the Steam Machine and its other upcoming hardware products are facing a delay, but not the way gamers might expect. Players can go to Steam right now and grabArabian Gulffor free, a single-playersurvival action gamereleased by indie developer IVDev in 2025. It is the first and only title by the developer. According to the game's Steam description, players will "face relentless enemies, endure tough conditions, and fight through dangerous terrain using basic tools and weapons." The objective here is to "survive, overcome each challenge, reach the distant castle, and claim the hidden treasure" in what is described as "a test of your skill and courage." At the time of writing, there aren't too many Steam player reviews forArabianGulf, though the ones that are there are mixed.
