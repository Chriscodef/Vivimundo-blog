Epic Games Forces Fortnite Cheater to Issue Public Apology

Epic Games has taken legal action against a player who was found to be cheating inFortnite, banning the offender in both the popular battle royale and the company's wider ecosystem.Fortniteis currently in the middle of Chapter 7 Season 1, which has featured a wide variety of crossovers with properties likeKill Bill,Back to the Future,South Park, and more. The battle royale's latest update introduced a collaboration betweenFortniteand Chappell Roan, a popular singer who won Best New Artist at the Grammy Awards in 2025. While gamers can purchase some of Chappell Roan's cosmetics in the Item Shop, players who want everything will need to pick up the Music Pass tied to the game mode Fortnite Festival. The collaboration includes new skins, jam tracks, emotes, and even a sidekick inspired by the artist's hit song "Pink Pony Club." Even though Epic Games tends to keep things light and fun with its collaborations, the company has taken a much more serious approach to addressing cheating within the live-service title. Fortnite is hinting at an unexpected crossover with Honkai: Star Rail arriving to the game soon, potentially teasing some featured characters. In a recent post on Twitter, Epic Games issued an update on its effort toeliminate cheating inFortnite. The post claims that Epic Games found and took legal action against a player who had been cheating and carrying out DDoS attacks onFortnite's servers. At the bottom of the post, Epic Games included a statement put out by ELVARFN, the player who was caught cheating. In the post, ELVARFN apologizes for their actions and fully admits to both cheating and engaging in actions that "targeted players and disrupted their games." The disgraced player then goes on to say that they "understand that this conduct is against the law and breaks Fortnite’s rules."
